## Section 5: Open Feedback
Optional questions to help us learn what's working and where we can improve.

### What's something GitHub is doing well when it comes to accessibility, caregiving support, or flexibility?
Analysis from Google Forms:
- **Flexibility and Remote Work:** The flexible schedule, remote-first/asynchronous work culture, and flexible working locations are widely praised, significantly helping with both personal medical needs and caregiving responsibilities. Several respondents note the ease of adjusting work times and taking necessary time off without worrying about running out of vacation time, with one person mentioning the ability to lie down as needed without notice.
- **Manager and Team Support:** Managerial understanding and support are vital for accommodations and flexibility, with respondents reporting that their managers are extremely accommodating regarding doctor appointments, medical needs, and adjustments.
- **ERGs and Community:** Support for Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) like Neurocats is highlighted as transformative, providing community and aiding in personal well-being.
- **Accessible Tools and Culture:** GitHub is doing well with accessible tooling, such as colorblind-friendly themes and ensuring presentation slides are visible during remote meetings. The culture of acknowledging that people have different needs and the practice of writing things down when possible are also appreciated.

### What's one thing that would make your work life easier or more accessible?
Analysis from Google Forms:
- **Normalization of Flexible/Asynchronous Work:** Requests included continuing to allow asynchronous work, normalizing being camera-off in meetings, and making GitHub more async overall, particularly for calls that lack transcription and documentation. Conversely, sometimes short face calls are more efficient than lengthy documents. AI summary tools and requiring TLDRs/clear action items in documents were suggested to also help.
- **Accommodations and Accessibility Resources:** Suggested improvements include raising the visibility of the existing disability accommodations hub or creating one specifically for GitHub, offering an enhanced mental health benefit or "accessibility budget," and simplifying the process for obtaining simple accommodations for off-site events.
- **Support for Work-Life Balance and Time Off:** Multiple respondents requested more public support and standardization for flexible/reduced hours and taking time off, noting that the "unlimited vacation" policy can be difficult to use due to feelings of guilt/shame; one person suggested an automated system to prompt taking more days off. There was also a call for Director-level and above leaders to better model healthy work-life balance.
- **Addressing Challenges with Synchronous Events/Offsites:** Concerns were raised over too many synchronous events (calls/offsites) only during US working hours, which complicates attendance for those with extensive travel times or caregiving responsibilities. Suggestions for offsites included holding space for rest, providing slides online, and organizing events closer to those who cannot travel.
- **Neurodiversity and Location Flexibility:** There is a need for greater managerial and leadership awareness of neurodiversity and its varied challenges. Additionally, one respondent requested less stringent geo-fencing (e.g., 20 miles) to access quiet focus rooms via Desana resources.
